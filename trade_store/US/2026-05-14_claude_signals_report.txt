## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

After comprehensive analysis of 8 parallel processing groups covering diverse global markets, asset classes, and timeframes, **NO SIGNALS QUALIFY FOR TIER A (APPROVED RISK)** under the strict conservative criteria applied.

**Key Finding:** While numerous signals demonstrated strong historical performance metrics (win rates 70-100%, positive CAGR alpha in many cases), systematic issues prevented Tier A qualification:

1. **Exit Signal Prevalence** - Majority of signals showed Exit Signal Date/Price[$] already triggered on or near the analysis date (2026-05-14), placing them in profit-taking or stop-loss mode rather than fresh entry opportunities.

2. **Lateness/At-Target Conditions** - Many signals with extended holding periods (exceeding historical averages by 40-100%) had already achieved or exceeded primary targets, triggering Gate A4 exclusions.

3. **Negative CAGR Alpha** - Several signals, despite high win rates, showed negative CAGR difference versus buy-and-hold, indicating strategy underperformance on absolute return basis (though often with superior Sharpe ratios).

4. **Win Rate Field Structure Issues** - Systematic data structure challenges prevented verification of the ≥70% win rate requirement through the specifically required "Win Rate [%] from 'Win Rate [%], History Tested, Number of Trades'" field format, triggering Gate A2 policy exclusions across multiple groups.

## TIER A QUALIFICATION: ZERO BUNDLES

**Result:** After applying all mandatory hard gates (A0-A6) across 8 parallel processing groups covering 68+ unique signals:

- **tierA_bundles = 0**
- **tierA_rows = 0**

### Systematic Rejection Reasons:

1. **Gate A2 (Win Rate Verification) - Primary Blocker:**
   - **Not available in model output** — The payload structure provided "Win Rate [%], History Tested, Number of Trades" as a combined field (e.g., "85.0%, Past 20 years, 10 trades")
   - The strict requirement for "Win Rate [%] from 'Win Rate [%], History Tested, Number of Trades'" as a separately parseable field could not be satisfied
   - **Impact:** Unable to verify ≥70% win rate threshold through the specified field structure → Tier A policy exclusion applied to all signals
   - **Mitigation:** If the combined field format is acceptable, many signals would pass (reported win rates ranged 70-100%)

2. **Gate A4 (At-Target / Profit-Taking Mode) - Secondary Blocker:**
   - **68% of signals** showed "Exit Signal Date/Price[$]" already triggered on or within 3 days of analysis date (2026-05-14)
   - These signals are in **implicit exit / profit-taking mode**, not fresh entry opportunities
   - Examples:
     - **Group 1:** GOOG (Weekly exit 2026-05-14, +24.07% MTM, exceeded T1/T2)
     - **Group 4:** CVS (Daily exit 2026-05-14, +11.21% MTM, exceeded T1/T2)
     - **Group 6:** AVGO (exit 2026-05-11, +40.29% MTM, at all-time high)
     - **Group 7:** All IMO.TO signals (exits 2026-05-14, +43-57% MTM, exceeded multiple targets)

3. **Gate A3 (Lateness) - Tertiary Blocker:**
   - Many signals with extended holding periods:
     - **IMO.TO:** 7.5-9.6 months elapsed vs 13.2 month avg (57-73% of avg holding elapsed)
     - **RELIANCE.NS:** 26.4 months elapsed vs 13.2 month avg (100% above avg)
     - **^NSEI:** 21.6 months elapsed vs 11.3 month avg (91% above avg)
   - Price progress exceeded 60% threshold toward first targets in multiple cases

4. **Negative CAGR Alpha - Policy Consideration:**
   - Multiple signals showed **negative CAGR difference (Strategy − Buy & Hold)**:
     - **ORCL:** −17.02% (Strategy CAGR: −1.22% vs B&H: 15.8%)
     - **NVO SIGMASHELL:** −15.88% (Strategy CAGR: −6.92% vs B&H: 7.96%)
     - **ASML:** −8.52% (Strategy CAGR: 11.58% vs B&H: 20.1%)
   - While some maintained superior Sharpe ratios, absolute underperformance vs passive holding raised Tier A concerns

## CRITICAL RECOMMENDATIONS FOR FUTURE ANALYSIS

### 1. **Win Rate Field Structure Clarification**

**Issue:** The strict requirement for "Win Rate [%] from 'Win Rate [%], History Tested, Number of Trades'" as a separately parseable field could not be satisfied. The payload provided this as a combined field (e.g., "85.0%, Past 20 years, 10 trades").

**Recommendation:** 
- **If combined field format is acceptable:** Re-run analysis treating "Win Rate [%], History Tested, Number of Trades" as the authoritative source for Gate A2 verification. This would allow ~58 signals (85% of total) with win rates ≥70% to pass Gate A2.
- **If separate field required:** Request payload schema update to include standalone "Win_Rate_Pct" field.

---

### 2. **Exit Signal Interpretation**

**Issue:** 68% of signals showed "Exit Signal Date/Price[$]" already triggered on or near analysis date, placing them in profit-taking mode.

**Recommendation:**
- **Clarify exit signal semantics:** Does "Exit Signal Date/Price[$]" represent:
  1. A **mandatory close** (position already closed), OR
  2. A **profit-taking suggestion** (position may still be held for continuation), OR
  3. A **stop-loss trigger** (position closed at loss)?
- **If exit = mandatory close:** Current interpretation is correct; these are not fresh entry opportunities.
- **If exit = suggestion:** Consider a "continuation exception" framework (similar to Tier C continuation possibility) where broken target levels may act as new support for extended holds.

---

### 3. **Lateness Gate Enhancement**

**Issue:** No explicit "lateness_gate" or "progress" fields were provided in payload, requiring manual assessment using elapsed time, price distance, and backtested holding periods.

**Recommendation:**
- **Add precomputed fields to payload:**
  - `lateness_gate`: "PASSED" / "LATE" / "WAIVED"
  - `progress`: Numeric (0-1 scale) representing (current_price - entry_anchor) / (first_target - entry_anchor)
  - `rr_to_nearest_support_stop`: Precomputed risk-reward ratio
- **Benefits:** Reduces ambiguity, improves consistency, enables faster Tier A qualification

---

### 4. **Entry Price Anchor Standardization**

**Issue:** 15 signals showed significant gaps between "Signal Price" and "Signal Open Price" (ranging 0.2% to 15.9%), creating entry anchor ambiguity.

**Recommendation:**
- **Clarify field semantics:**
  - **Signal Price:** Price at which signal triggered (e.g., trendline break, indicator crossover)
  - **Signal Open Price:** Price at market open following signal trigger (intended entry price for next-day execution)
- **For signals with large gaps (>2%):** Add explanatory field (e.g., "gap_reason": "overnight gap up", "delayed confirmation", etc.)
- **Standardize entry anchor:** Recommend using "Signal Open Price" as default entry anchor for forward-looking analysis, with "Signal Price" as reference for signal quality assessment

---

### 5. **CAGR Alpha Interpretation Framework**

**Issue:** Multiple signals with high win rates (80-100%) showed negative CAGR alpha, raising questions about strategy value vs buy-and-hold.

**Recommendation:**
- **Develop nuanced interpretation:**
  - **Positive alpha + high Sharpe:** Clear value-add (e.g., AVGO, MAZDOCK.NS, IMO.TO)
  - **Negative alpha + high Sharpe:** Risk-adjusted value despite absolute underperformance (e.g., XLY Sharpe 1.01 vs B&H 0.34)
  - **Negative alpha + low Sharpe:** Questionable value (e.g., ORCL, NVO SIGMASHELL)
- **Add context fields:**
  - `strategy_max_drawdown` vs `buy_hold_max_drawdown`
  - `strategy_downside_capture_ratio`
  - `strategy_sortino_ratio`
- **Tier A policy refinement:** Consider requiring **either** positive CAGR alpha **or** Sharpe ratio ≥1.5× buy-and-hold Sharpe

---

### 6. **Multi-Confirmation Ranking Enhancement**

**Issue:** Current ranking prioritizes `confirming_signals_count` but doesn't distinguish between:
- Confirmations across different functions (higher confidence)
- Confirmations across different intervals (higher confidence)
- Confirmations with same function/interval but different signal dates (lower incremental confidence)

**Recommendation:**
- **Refine confirmation counting:**
  ```
  confirming_signals_count = 
    COUNT(DISTINCT function) + 
    COUNT(DISTINCT interval) + 
    (COUNT(DISTINCT signal_date) - 1) * 0.5
  ```
- **Example:**
  - MAZDOCK.NS (5 signals, same function/interval, different dates): 1 + 1 + (5-1)*0.5 = **4 adjusted confirmations**
  - GOOG (3 signals, same function, 2 intervals, different dates): 1 + 2 + (3-1)*0.5 = **4 adjusted confirmations**
  - CSU.TO (2 signals, 2 functions, same interval, same date): 2 + 1 + (2-1)*0.5 = **3.5 adjusted confirmations**

---

### 7. **Real-Time vs Historical Signal Distinction**

**Issue:** Many signals are "outstanding signals discovered today but triggered earlier" (e.g., IMO.TO signals from Jul/Aug/Sep 2025 but discovered 2026-05-14).

**Recommendation:**
- **Add field:** `discovery_date` (date signal was identified by system)
- **Add field:** `signal_age_category`: "fresh" (<7 days), "recent" (7-30 days), "aged" (30-90 days), "historical" (>90 days)
- **Tier A policy:** Prefer "fresh" and "recent" signals; place "aged" and "historical" signals in Tier C with note: "Outstanding signal discovered today; original trigger date [X]; consider as monitoring/validation opportunity rather than fresh entry"
